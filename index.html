GTM strategy & revenue model for a broken advisory

3.87xLTV:CAC (from 0.64x)
$284CAC (from $471)
$1,100LTV (from $300)
+$43KMonth-24 cash

The problem

The client ran a VC advisory for founders who couldn't raise. Natural market. Broken economics: $471 CAC, $300 LTV, 0.64x ratio, burning cash by month 24. The ask was qualitative — "we're not converting well." The real issues were structural: an unstandardised product, no trust infrastructure, and an unfiltered qualification funnel absorbing human time on calls that went nowhere.

My job was to quantify it, name the levers, and build a model that answered the actual question: what does fixing this look like, and what does it cost?

Diagnosis: four levers

After mapping the full acquisition funnel — cold outbound, IRL meetups, VC rejection volume — the fix came down to four things: productise the IP into a repeatable framework, rebuild website authority around real expertise, automate lead filtering with a scoring form built on that framework, and a minimal content tier for thought leadership. Each lever had a modelled impact on CAC, LTV, and human time cost.

The channel choice (cold outbound) is correct for a high-value service. The problem was running a high-CAC channel against a low-LTV product. Productising the IP to reach $1,000+ LTV is what makes the channel work.

The model

Built a live revenue model covering all four levers across 18 configurations — LTV configs from $300 to $1,100, content tiers from signal-only to heavy investment, engagement level from none to $3,000/mo fractional. Every number is a toggle, not a fixed assumption.

Optimised scenario: 3.87x CAC:LTV, positive cash by month 24. The largest single variable — automatically converting VC rejection volume into advisory leads — is likely underestimated in the model and could be the difference-maker.

Hotspots: US Dams Under Pressure

Of the 91,000 dams in the United States — averaging 57 years old — 15,500 were classified as extremely hazardous in 2018. This piece maps Texan dams against extreme temperature projections from the Max Planck Institute climate model, showing which structures could face 45°C+ heat events under 2°C warming, and 50°C+ under 4°C. The rehabilitation bill: over $65 billion.

Built using Python, QGIS, and Adobe Illustrator.

Shifting Baselines and the Elusiveness of Climate Change

Each generation inherits a degraded environment as its baseline, erasing the memory of what came before. Using 60 years of German weather data, this piece shows how average hot days rose from 4 per year for Baby Boomers to 9 for Gen Z, while frost days fell by 16 across the same period — a generational amnesia that slows the collective response to climate change.

Built using Python and Adobe Illustrator.
