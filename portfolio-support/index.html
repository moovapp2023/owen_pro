GTM strategy & revenue model for a broken advisory

A VC advisory firm with unsustainable unit economics — $471 CAC, $300 LTV, 0.64x ratio, and a qualification process burning human time on calls that went nowhere. Came in to quantify the problem, diagnose the levers, and build a live revenue model charting the path to profitability.

The problem

The client ran a VC advisory for founders who couldn't raise. Natural market, broken economics: $471 CAC, $300 LTV, 0.64x ratio, burning cash by month 24. The real issues were structural — an unstandardised product, no trust infrastructure, and a qualification funnel that was absorbing partner and associate time on calls that went nowhere.

The ask was qualitative: "we're not converting well." My job was to quantify it, name the levers, and build a model that answered the actual question: what does fixing this look like, and what does it cost?

Diagnosis: four levers

After mapping the full acquisition funnel — cold outbound, IRL meetups, VC rejection volume — the fix came down to four things: productise the IP into a repeatable framework, rebuild website authority around real expertise, automate lead filtering with a scoring form, and a minimal content tier for thought leadership. Each lever had a modelled impact on CAC, LTV, and human time cost.

Channel choice (cold outbound) is correct for a high-value service. The problem was running a high-CAC channel against a low-LTV product. Productising the IP to reach $1,000+ LTV is what makes the channel work.

The model

Built a live revenue model covering all four levers across 18 configurations — LTV configs from $300 to $1,100, content tiers from signal-only to heavy investment, engagement level from none to $3,000/mo fractional. Every number is a toggle, not a fixed assumption.

Optimised scenario: 3.87x CAC:LTV, positive cash by month 24. The largest single variable — automatically converting VC rejection volume into advisory leads — is likely underestimated and could be the difference-maker.
