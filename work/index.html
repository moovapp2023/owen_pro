The Scanlon extrapolation

The headline figure, 250 years of viable extraction remaining, is an original calculation built on depletion rate projections from Scanlon et al. (2012) and CVHM2 hydrological basin modeling. Satellite GRACE data provides the empirical depletion curve.

Earth.org  ·  Dataviz

Hotspots: US Dams Under Pressure

The US has around 91,000 dams with an average age of 57 years, 15,500 of which were classed as extremely hazardous in 2018. Using MPI-GE climate simulations, this piece maps Texan dams by age and projected peak temperature exposure under 2°C and 4°C global warming scenarios — visualising a largely invisible infrastructure risk.

Earth.org  ·  Data journalism

Shifting Baselines and the Elusiveness of Climate Change

Each generation accepts the climate they are born into as normal, which is why the gradual heating of the planet goes largely unfelt. This piece charts yearly hot days in Germany across four generations — Baby Boomers through Gen Z — using German weather service data to make the shifting baseline visible.

Extreme Temperatures – Part II: France

Part of a series modelling the most extreme temperatures possible under 2–4°C warming using MPI-GE climate simulations. This piece focuses on France — mapping heat stress thresholds against dairy cattle tolerance and overlaying wine regions to show which appellations face existential risk.
